Watches have been around for some time now. To give you a brief idea of how long, the first watch was invented in 1505, by a man who goes by the name of Peter Henlein. Mr Henlein was a locksmith and clockmaker from Nuremberg, Germany. Though, the first wrist watch was made in 1868. It was made by a Swiss watch manufacturer called Patek Philippe.

Over time the function of a wrist watch has evolved like most things, but the number one main purpose has not changed. That purpose is to keep track of time. But nowadays wrist watches have many uses and purposes. To be brief I'll name a few uses:

  • Fashion Accessory
    Having a wrist watch can take a 5/10 outfit and change it into a 7 or 8/10 outfit.Most people
    nowadays use a watch to compliment or elavate their outfit.

  • Form of Status
    Like most material things, wrist watches also have an element of giving a person a certain status
    in society or in their community. This is due to the fact that watches are not the same therefore they
    won't cost the same. If a person is wearing a Patek Philippe opposed to another person who is
    wearing a Casio, the person with the Patek will have a 'greater' status. Just because the Patek cost
    more.

  • Investment
    If you are lucky enough to own a wrist watch which is considered an antique, that same wrist watch
    can be worth double what you got it for in 5-10 years.

  • Technological Device
    Over time the wrist watch evolved and became more and more digital. Modern watches have
    the ability to monitor your heart rate and many other modern feature. Other smart watches can
    sync with your cell device, that helps with reading notifications if your cell device is far from you.

The Patek Philippe wrist watch is a Swiss luxury watch and clock manufacturer. Patek Philippe was founded in 1839. It is reported that the Swiss based company, which is owned by the Stern family in Switzerland, produces 60,000 timepiece annually.

Founded in London by two men, Hans Wilsdorf and Alfred Davis. The Swiss based watch manufacturer underwent various name changes, such as; Wilsdorf and Davis, Rolex Watch Co.Ltd and Montres Rolex SA. Until it was eventually decided that the Swiss watch would be named Rolex SA.
